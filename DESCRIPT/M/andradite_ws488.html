File: andr488,out,*_mdi (smear mount on quartz plate)
References: Borg and Smith (1969), JCPDS #10-288, DHZ, v. 1a (1982), Huebner's reference patterns
Found: Garnet; five un indexed weak to very weak reflections.

Comment: Well crystallized, but not exceptional. Additional reflections at 7.1, 3.55, 2.84, 1.63 Angstroms. Garnet ao=11.93+/-3 Angstroms, indicating presence of andradite or uvarovite in solid solution. Based on brownish color, concluded that this garnet is andradite component in solid solution with Gr, Sp, Alm, or Py, most likely grossular.

J.S. Huebner, J. Pickrell, T. Schaefer, written communication 1994

MICROSCOPIC_EXAMINATION:

Optical examination gives the following mineral mode:

96 vol% andradite
4 vol% opaques (biotite?) not magnetite

Bimodal grain size distribution:

population 1 avg. grain size = 150um vol%= 75
population 2 avg. grain size = 10um vol%= 25%

avg. grain size of all populations = 130um

Conchoidally fractured andradite grains with opaque inclusions in the grains. 10um sized andradite grains coat 60% of the surfaces of the larger grains. Large grain surfaces either conchoidal fractures or flat parting surfaces. No fizz from HCl. G. Swayze
